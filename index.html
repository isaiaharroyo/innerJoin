The way the SQL INNER JOIN keyword works is that it searches through two different tables and displays a table that contains the information that is found in both tables. The Syntax of INNER JOIN is as follows:

SELECT [names of columns that will be in table]

FROM [name of first table]

INNER JOIN [name of second table]

ON [table 1].[name of column you want searched] = [table 2].[column name]
